AIMS AND OBJECTIVES OF THE CAMEROON BIOSCIENCE SOCIETY (CBS)

CBS is a non-profit organization that was established in 1988, with the following general objectives:

To promote communication among scientists in the field of biosciences at national and international levels.

To organize conferences, lectures, workshops and discussions for the promotion of the sciences.

To promote collaboration with other related organizations in the areas of research, education, training and publications.

To publish journals, proceedings, and other works as may be considered desirable.

To accept grants, donations or endowments from Governments, other organizations or individuals for projects or purposes which do not violate the spirit of this constitution such as the award of fellowships, prizes, grants or medals for the promotion of research and development in the area of Bioscience.

To undertake such other activities as may assist in or be necessary for the fulfilment of the above mentioned objectives.

CAMEROON BIOSCIENCES FELLOWSHIP

CBS offers fellowships to enable graduate students/postdoctoral fellows/researchers to attend and present their research findings at the Annual CBS Conference and/or participate at the CBS Pre-conference workshops.
There are two categories of fellowships:

  • A-1 CBS Annual Conference Fellowship
  • A-2 CBS-Preconference Workshops Fellowship
Selection of the successful candidate is based on the following criteria:

B-1 Criteria for the CBS Annual Conference Fellowship:.

  • the applicant should be a graduate or doctoral student / researcher working in the field of Biosciences or related disciplines;
  • preference will be given to researchers below 35 years of age;
  • the applicant must be a paid-up member of CBS;
  • the applicant has submitted an application by the due date, providing all requested information and documentation;
  • preference will be given to those applicants who have not previously received a CBS Annual Fellowship
